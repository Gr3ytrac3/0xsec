I spend a lot of time thinking about the offensive security of systems at the lowest levels — kernels, hypervisors, and the boundaries between them. I use this site to share research, notes, and findings that might be useful to others working on similar problems.

---


**Hey, I'm  Cyberdev.**

I'm a self-taught security researcher and software engineer who spends most of his time digging into Linux internals, kernel exploitation, and the kind of low-level systems stuff that most people walk past without noticing.

I started out just curious. Not with a roadmap or a degree — just a laptop, a lot of broken VMs, and questions that kept leading to more questions. That's still pretty much how it goes.

This site is where I write about what I'm learning and building. Some of it is deep technical research, some of it is debugging sessions that took way too long and taught me something useful. All of it is written the way I wish someone had explained it to me when I was starting out.

**If you're a beginner** — you belong here. You don't need to understand everything on first read. Neither did I.

**If you're experienced** — let's dig in together. I'm always open to feedback, corrections, and good technical arguments.

I work on things like kernel security, covert channel research, reverse engineering, and vulnerability research under the handle **Gr3ytrac3**, affiliated with **The OffSec Desk**.

You can find me on [GitHub](https://github.com/Gr3ytrac3), [Substack](https://substack.com/@cyberdevhq), and [X](https://x.com/thecyberdevhq) — or just reach out directly if something you read here sparked a question.

_Curiosity first. Always._
